I am a project manager at a large aerospace company. During my front-end web development certification at UW Professional & Continuing Education, I was taught about the keys to responsive design.

Design Philosophy

Web design is all about drawing customers in with streamlined and effective websites. Websites should be accessible for all types of users, regardless of web browser, operating system or monitor size.

Those with vision, cognitition or physical impairments are included in this web accessbility philosophy, as they may consist of 20% of all users.

A responive website also affects customers without disabilities, such as those with slow internet or situational limitations like glaring sunlight or broken arms.

During my front-end web development certification at UW Professional & Continuing Education, I was taught the keys to responsive design, including mobile first design, fluid layouts, flexible grid and media queries.

Accessibility overlaps with other best practices such as mobile web design, device independence, multi-modal interaction, usability, design for older users, and search engine optimization. Accessible websites can have better search results, reduced maintenance costs, increased audience reach, and demonstrate corporate social responsibility.

Web accessibility is required by law in many situations.
